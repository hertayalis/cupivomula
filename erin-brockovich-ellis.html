    Age, Biography and Wiki

    Erin Brockovich-Ellis is an American legal clerk and environmental activist who, despite her lack of formal education in the law, was instrumental in building a case against the Pacific Gas and Electric Company of California in 1993. Her successful lawsuit was the subject of a 2000 film, Erin Brockovich, which starred Julia Roberts. Erin Brockovich-Ellis was born on June 22, 1960, in Lawrence, Kansas. She was raised in Lawrence and attended Lawrence High School. After graduating, she moved to California, where she worked as a management trainee at Kmart. In 1982, Brockovich-Ellis married Shawn Brown, with whom she had two children. The couple divorced in 1987. In 1989, she married Steven Brockovich, with whom she had three children. The couple divorced in 1991. In 1993, Brockovich-Ellis was hired as a legal clerk at the law firm of Masry & Vititoe. While working there, she discovered a link between the Pacific Gas and Electric Company and a cluster of illnesses in the town of Hinkley, California. She worked with the firm to build a case against the company, which resulted in a $333 million settlement. In 2000, the film Erin Brockovich was released, starring Julia Roberts as Brockovich-Ellis. The film was a critical and commercial success, and earned Roberts an Academy Award for Best Actress. Today, Brockovich-Ellis is an environmental activist and public speaker. She is the president of Brockovich Research & Consulting, a consulting firm that specializes in environmental issues. She is also the founder of the Erin Brockovich Foundation, which works to protect the environment and public health. As of 2021, Erin Brockovich-Ellis's net worth is estimated to be roughly $50 million.
